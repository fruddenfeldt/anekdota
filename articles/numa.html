Numa

After Romulus came Numa, the second King of Rome. If Romulus was the warrior-leader, Numa was the pacifist-priest.

According to the legend, Numa, or Numa Pomilius, is credited with creating many of Rome's most important religious instituions, including the office of the Pontifex Maximus.

He also managed to keep Rome out of war. The temple of Juno (?), built during his reign, had its doors shut his whole reign, which never happened again.
