The rise of Rome is still a mystery.

It was a mystery even to the Romans themselves. Rome lasted for a very long time, and the written record was very scant, but Romans needed a story on how they came about.

There are legends, there are theories about those legends. There are also archeological finds, and theories about those finds.

There is no firm evidence for any of those.

The legends, however, were what the Romas told themselves about their origins. This itself makes them interesting.

The strangest thing about these legends is how unflattering they are. To take a few examples:

  • Remulus killed his brother in what seems like an unecessary act of rage.
  • Early Rome lacked women so they abducted them from neighboring towns.
  • Several of the later, but still legendary, Kings were not from Rome but Etruria
  • Ex-slaves were common among senior leaders

What can we take away from this period when looking at Rome's later (and more well-documented) history?

  • Social mobility was an accepted contept from the beginning
  • Using violence to get a high position was common early on
  • Seeking to become 'King' become a strong political taboo.
