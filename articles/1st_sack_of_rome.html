The first sack of Rome

Around 300 before Christ, Rome was invaded by a Gaulish tribe.

After this, Rome would not be invaded by a foreign force for another 800 (?) years - a remarkable feat in ancient history, or perhaps any history.

At the time of the invasion, Rome was still not the empire that we think of today. It was a powerful but not yet dominant regional force; Rome didn't even control all of Italy. The city had, however, forged strong alliances across Northern Italy.
